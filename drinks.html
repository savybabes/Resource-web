Jamaican Rum Punch

Rum punch was invented in Jamaica by Wray & Nephew as early as the mid-1980s. Originally named 'Sugar Wray Rum Punch,' the drink's name was later simplified. In Jamaica, rum punch is a celebratory beverage commonly enjoyed at weddings, funerals, family gatherings, birthday parties, and other special occasions.

Sorrel

Sorrel was introduced to Jamaica in the 18th century from West Africa. This drink is made from the red-skinned hibiscus plant, brought across the Middle Passage by enslaved Africans. Red foods and beverages hold cultural significance for freed Black people, symbolizing resilience and celebration. African Americans also incorporate them into Juneteenth traditions each year to honour emancipation.
